Under the hood — Why did I build this and how

I've spent the last five years building GTM motions for CX solutions across financial services, insurance, retail, telco, and healthcare.

I started at Qelp, a startup building self-service tools for customer support. We got acquired by SYKES, where I moved into business development for their automation suite covering RPA and analytics.

When SYKES merged into Foundever, I shifted into global strategy, owning how the entire managed service portfolio went to market across EMEA.

This experience gave me my MBA in CX by understanding the whole ecosystem of customer support, from self-service automation to embedded large operations. This is why I know I can bring value as I understand the whole CX proposition for each of this verticals.

A few things worth knowing about me

  • I've been part of campaigns that influenced over 400M in revenue
  • I built sales enablement and lead gen formats that helped open healthcare as a net new vertical and define the main ICP
  • I've researched this vertical, workshoped with SMEs and launched campaigns to understand insurers are tired of being sold AI that doesn't stick and is non-compliant

That last one is exactly why I built this page. When I looked at how ElevenLabs was showing up in insurance, two things stood out:

  1. ElevenLabs was positioning insurance either as a generic AI answering service or folding it into a broader financial services bucket. No vertical depth, no language that resonates with someone running claims ops or underwriting.
  2. ElevenLabs received AIUC-1 certification on insurance agents one month ago. That's a meaningful proof point sitting completely unused in their positioning.

So I asked a different question: where does ElevenLabs actually win in insurance, and what would a page look like that was built by someone who knows the vertical? Here's where I landed:

  • Insurers want to deflect the predictable, high-volume call drivers around policy queries, claims intake, and underwriting so their human agents can focus on conversations that actually require judgment
  • Every inconsistency in a FNOL call is a potential fraud exposure. A voice agent that captures structured data every single time, with a full audit trail, isn't a pilot. It's infrastructure.
  • The opportunity is positioning ElevenLabs not as another AI tool to test, but as the layer that makes compliance and conversion work together

That's what I built here.
